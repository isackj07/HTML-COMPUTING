Overview

This hands-on case study investigates how quickly someone with no foundation in
typing can improve in touch typing when compared against someone with experience
in freehand typing in terms of typing speed and accuracy, over a
structured 5 day training program. We evaluate improvement rate, error rates,and
consistency. Through structured daily practice, based on metrics such as words per
minute (WPM), and typing accuracy, we aim to gain insights into learning curves,
plateau phases, and motivational aspects of touch typing.

What is blind typing?

Blind typing, also known as touch typing, is the skill of typing on a keyboard without looking at the keyboard. It involves memorizing the position of keys and relying on muscle memory to type quicky and accurately. This method improves typing speed and efficiency, as it allows users to focus on the screen. Blind typing is commonly taught through practice exercises and typing software, and it's a valuable skill for students, professionals, and anyone who uses a computer regularly.
