Data collection

1. Quantitative Data

Quantitative data provides measurable and objective evidence to evaluate typing performance over time.
The following key metrics are essential for tracking progress and validating our findings

  • WPM SCORE

    • This measures typing speed, indicating how many words a person can type within a minute.
    It's a critical benchmark for assessing improvement, especially when comparing the performance of beginners versus experienced typists.
    Increases in WPM directly reflect enhanced typing fluency and efficiency.

  • TYPING ACCURACY

    • Accuracy represents the percentage of correctly typed characters or words. A high WPM score is meaningless without accuracy; therefore,
    this metric ensures that speed is not achieved at the cost of correctness. It's essential for identifying whether users are typing quickly and correctly.

  • ERROR RATE

    • Error rate measures the frequency of mistakes made during typing. It helps identify patterns in errors, such as common typos or miskeyed letters.
    A lower error rate indicates better mastery of the keyboard layout and improved muscle memory.


Strategies for Minimizing Errors

1. Proper technique

Using correct posture, finger placement, and hand positioning is essential for building accurate muscle memory.
Without proper technique, participants may develop bad habits that lead to more errors and hinder progress.
Ensuring a solid foundation helps users type more accurately and consistently over time.

2. Psychological

Mental state plays a significant role in typing performance. Fatigue, stress, or frustration can cause inconsistency
and higher error rates during sessions. In short, the more you type in this state, the more likely you are to make mistakes.
This is also known as being "tilted".

3. Applying uniform measurement standards to assess development

Consistent use of standardized metrics (e.g., WPM, accuracy, error rate) ensures that all participants are evaluated fairly and objectively.
Changing the way performance is measured between sessions can introduce errors or bias into the analysis. By sticking to the same evaluation criteria,
we can track genuine progress and compare results accurately across participants and time.
